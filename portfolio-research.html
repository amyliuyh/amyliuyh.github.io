Built Environment Experience Assessment and Design via Bio-Feedback

Research & paper, National Science Foundation of China

Overview

In this research, we try to introduce a new technique, affective mapping assessment based on bio feedback, which can be used on environment experience evaluation and lead us to the right design strategy.

Design Thinking Process
Reveals the deficiency of environment space design: it lacks technical approach and user testing.


Experiment Instruments

An 8-channeled Procomp Infiniti device was used in this study, recording electrocardiogram (ECG), electroencephalogram (EEG), skin conductance (SC), skin temperature (ST), electromyography (EMG) of facial muscles expression and respiration, Physiological measures were first filtered and then combined with GPS locations and visual eyesight.






Outdoor environment measurement

The participants were asked to take a 15-minute walk across Tongji University campus in Shanghai with the instruments.





Interview

Phenomenological Interview
After interview to the participants, the following map can be drown to present the general emotional distributions. We can see 6 makeable zones on the map. The pleasant emotions mainly distribute in zone 1, 5, and 6, whereas people felt unpleasant in zone 2, 3, 4.

Psychological Scales
Based on the grades the participants scored, a map has been drown to show the assessment of the 8 spots. Red for arousal and green for valence, the higher the grades are, the deeper the color is.

The map indicates that in Spot 1, 7 and 8 the grades of both arousal and valence are high, which means those spots brought mainly positive influence on the participants’ emotion.
2，5 don’t cause clear changes in mood.
Other spots are all higher in arousal than valence which means they bring negative stimuli.






Bio-sensory measures

After thorough analysis of the usable datasets of each walk, they were depicted via the time- and GPS-stamp in a GIS-based heat map. 8 meaningful hot spots for emotional responses can be identified. 4 pleasant, 4 unpleasant.
Unpleasant emotions show comparably higher magnitudes than pleasant ones. Along the route, further minor responses can be found, both for positive and negative responses.

It shows the environmental stimulus to human feelings.






Comparison & Result

The comparison among the bio-sensory and the two traditional interview methods indicated that this new developed method is to some degree valid. This new method can reveal participants‘ emotional reaction to the environmental design effectively.






Design suggestion

Based on our new method, we can come up with certain design suggestion to improve current user experience.

We redesigned the space of pedestrian and parking by moving the trees toward the building, so pedestrian and cars won't interfere.






Our released paper

Built Environment Experience Assessment and Design via Bio-Feedback

Writing Sample

Abstract:
Wearable sensors, artificial intelligence, and internet of things are changing the interactions of us and future generations with our environment and others profoundly, and thus offering new opportunities and challenges for built environment design. This paper introduces a new quantification technical approach, affective mapping based on biological feedbacks. This technique uses mobile bio-feedback sensors such as skin conductance, electroencephalogram(EEG), eletrocardiogram(EKG), facial expression muscle electromyography(EMG), skin temperature, respiration, supported with geological position service(GPS) via satellite and Wi-Fi, to measure and assess environmental experience ourdoors and indoors. The technique facilitates identifying environmental stimuli which trigger positive and negative affection, and then lead to design renewal strategies.
Key words:
Experience conputing, Human factor design, Multi-sensory experience, Affective mapping, Built environment
Article number:
1005-684X(2017)05-0024-005




From Cognitive Behavior Science to Environmental Neuroscience: Evidence-based Design for In-situ Environmental Experience Augmentation.

Abstract:
Environmental experience is central to spatial planning and design, and is also the current focus of architectural and urban design. However, it is not easy to precisely measure human spatial experience. In this paper, we first reviewed three classical design analysis methods describing spatial experience based on cognitive behavior sciences. We then moved to the neuroscience methods and related implications in describing spatial experience, followed by our own research explorations. Neuroimaging techniques such as EEG and skin conductance have become a new international trend in design-aid techniques to reveal real-time environmental experience.

Keyword:
environmental neuroscience; in-situ environment; experience augmentation; evidence-based design
